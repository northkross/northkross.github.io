Notation

The following notation is used to describe moves on a Rubik's Cube:

Note: The apostrophe (') also called prime, indicates a counterclockwise rotation, and the number 2 indicates a 180-degree rotation.

  • F - Front face clockwise
  • B - Back face clockwise
  • L - Left face clockwise
  • R - Right face clockwise
  • U - Upper face clockwise
  • D - Lower face clockwise
  • F' - Front face counterclockwise
  • B' - Back face counterclockwise
  • L' - Left face counterclockwise
  • R' - Right face counterclockwise
  • U' - Upper face counterclockwise
  • D' - Lower face counterclockwise
  • F2 - Front face 180 degrees
  • B2 - Back face 180 degrees
  • L2 - Left face 180 degrees
  • R2 - Right face 180 degrees
  • U2 - Upper face 180 degrees
  • D2 - Lower face 180 degrees
  • x - Rotate the entire cube on the x-axis
  • y - Rotate the entire cube on the y-axis
  • z - Rotate the entire cube on the z-axis
  • M - Middle slice (between L and R)
  • E - Equatorial slice (between U and D)
  • S - Standing slice (between F and B)

Steps to Solve a Rubik's Cube

For Beginners(Beginner Method):

  1. Learn the basic notation and moves.
  2. Master the white cross.

    3. Place all the white edge pieces in a cross pattern with the red secondary color opposite to orange and blue opposite to green; have all the secondary colors match up to their center piece

  3. Solve the white corners.

    4. Place all the white corner pieces above the spot they belong in and one at a time use this algorithm up to six times: R, U, R', U'

  4. Complete the second layer.

    5. For second layer edge pieces; ie no white or yellow stickers, line up an edge piece color with the correct color center piece and depending on which way it needs to go use the following algorithms: For edges that need to go to the left: U', L', U, L, U, F, U', F'. For edges that need to go to the right: U, R, U', R', U', F', U, F.

  5. Form the yellow cross.

    6. Depending on the pattern of yellow pieces on the top face, use the following algorithm: For a dot pattern: F, R, U, R', U', F'. For an L shape(position the L in the top left corner of the cube): F, U, R, U', R', F'. For a line(long ways; from left to right): F, R, U, R', U', F'.

  6. Position the yellow corners.

    7. Use the following algorithm until all the yellow corners are in the correct position: U, R, U', L', U, R', U', L.

  7. Orient the final layer.

    8. Use the following algorithm one at a time, turning the U layer after each iteration until all the yellow pieces are facing up: R', D', R, D.

For Advanced Cubers(CFOP Method):

  1. Cross: Solve the cross on the first layer.

    2. Similar to the beginner method, but with more efficient algorithms and lookahead techniques to minimize cube rotations and improve speed.

  2. F2L (First Two Layers).

    3. Pair up the corner and edge pieces of the first two layers simultaneously, using intuitive or algorithmic methods to insert them into their correct positions; not much to learn here just practice and figure stuff out on your own

  3. OLL (Orientation of the Last Layer) algorithms.

    4. Learn the 57 algorithms to orient all the pieces of the last layer correctly, regardless of their position.

    OLL Algorithms
  4. PLL (Permutation of the Last Layer) techniques.

    5. Learn the 21 algorithms to permute the pieces of the last layer, placing them in their correct positions while maintaining their orientation.
