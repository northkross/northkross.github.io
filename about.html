    WCA

    The World Cube Association (WCA) is the international governing body for competitive speedcubing. It organizes and regulates official competitions around the world, sets the rules and standards for solving puzzles, and maintains a database of records and rankings. The WCA promotes fair play, encourages community engagement, and fosters the growth of speedcubing as a sport. Whether you're interested in participating in competitions or simply want to connect with other cubers, the WCA provides a platform for cubers of all levels to come together and celebrate their passion for speedcubing.

    About Speedcubing

    Speedcubing is the practice of solving a Rubik's Cube as quickly as possible. It has evolved into a competitive sport with world records being broken regularly. Enthusiasts around the globe participate in competitions, sharing techniques and pushing the boundaries of human dexterity and memory. The current world record single is 2.76 seconds by Teodor Zajder set in early 2026 and the world record average is Xuanyi Geng at 3.84 seconds also set in early 2026.

    Methods

    There are many different methods for speedcubing, each with their own unique approach and set of algorithms. Some popular methods include the Beginner's Method, CFOP method, Roux method, and ZZ method. Here's a description of each of the methods

    Beginner's Method

    The Beginner's Method is a simple approach to solving the Rubik's Cube, ideal for those just starting out. It involves solving the cube layer by layer, making it easy to learn but not the fastest method.

    CFOP Method

    The CFOP method, also known as the Fridrich method, is a popular speedcubing method that focuses on solving the cube in four steps: Cross, F2L (First Two Layers), OLL (Orientation of the Last Layer), and PLL (Permutation of the Last Layer). It is widely used by competitive cubers due to its efficiency and speed.

    Roux Method

    The Roux method is a speedcubing approach that involves building two 1x2x3 blocks on opposite sides of the cube, followed by solving the remaining pieces. It is known for its intuitive nature and reduced number of moves.

    ZZ Method

    The ZZ method is a speedcubing technique that starts with orienting all edges on the U and D faces, then proceeds to solve the cube using a combination of block-building and intuitive solving techniques.
